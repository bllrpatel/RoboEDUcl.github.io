Effects of Nicotine and MSG on Neural Activity

In this experiment students will test the eff ect of neuroactive compounds on the central nervous system. Students will first sedate a living cricket by placing it over ice, and attach electrodes to visualize the cricket's neural activity. After observing a baseline activity, students will inject the cricket with Nicotine and Monosodium Glutamate (MSG), and observe the effects of these drugs on the cricket's neural activity.

Measuring Reaction Time to Auditory and Visual Signals on Dominant versus Non-Dominant Hands

In this experiment students will measure each other's reaction time. Using the software provided, students will observe their very own EMG (Electromyography) signal. Students will then begin to trigger either a visual or auditory signal to which another student must flex their muscle too. In this way we can ascertain whether the human brain reacts faster to auditory or visual signals.

Introduction to Circuits and Computational Thinking

In this experiment students are introduced to simple concepts in Electricity and Magnetism which include the notion of a voltage, current, resistance, and naturally Ohm's Law. Students will also be introduced to computational thinking, a precursor to Computer Science. They will be taught the sequential nature of computer programs, and a few simple constructs such as if/then statements and while loops. Students will then proceed to use a customized implementation of Blockly (a visual programming language) to make a robot that steers based on students shining a light on it.

EMG Controlled Robotic Vehicle

In a final culminating experiment, students will integrate their knowledge from the day and engineer an EMG controlled robotic vehicle.
